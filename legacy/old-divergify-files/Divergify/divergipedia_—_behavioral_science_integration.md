## A

### Admin Alley

**n.** A small, predictable daily window reserved exclusively for the soul-crushing tasks you've been avoiding: emails, bills, forms, insurance paperwork, the mortgage refinance you started three months ago. Admin Alley corrals all the boring adult garbage into one containment zone so it doesn't leak into your creative hours. You're not procrastinating â€” you're scheduling strategically. Set it for 15â€“30 minutes, ideally at a low-energy time of day when you weren't going to write the Great American Novel anyway, and knock out two things maximum. The goal isn't to finish everything; it's to prove the pile isn't infinite.

**Behavioral principle:** *Environmental cue design* â€” by creating a dedicated time-space anchor for admin work, you reduce decision fatigue and eliminate the constant low-level dread that comes from having undone tasks floating around your mental workspace.

### Almost There Alert

**n.** A notification that fires when Divergify detects you're about 80% through a task â€” the exact moment when most ADHD brains lose interest and wander off to start something new. Almost There Alert gives you the nudge: "You're so close. Finish this one thing." It's the difference between 47 half-done projects and actually crossing a finish line. **Behavioral principle:** *Attention hijacking at critical drop-off points* â€” intervention timing is everything. By interrupting at the 80% mark (when motivation naturally wanes), the system redirects focus before momentum is lost.

### Anchor Task

**n.** The one task that, if completed, stabilizes your entire day â€” even if everything else falls apart. Anchor Task isn't necessarily the biggest or hardest task; it's the one that prevents the day from feeling like a total wash. It might be "send that one email," "do 10 minutes of the priority project," or "take a shower." On good days, Anchor Task is your launchpad. On rough days, it's your lifeline. Divergify surfaces your Anchor Task early and protects it from being buried under 47 smaller fires.

**Behavioral principle:** *Single-point commitment strategy* â€” by reducing the "successful day" definition to ONE achievable action, you eliminate the paralysis that comes from overwhelming task lists.

## E

### Ellipsis Effect

**n.** The psychological pull created by leaving something *almost* finished â€” a cliffhanger for your brain. Your nervous system hates incomplete patterns and will obsess over finishing them. Ellipsis Effect leverages this by never showing you the full task list at once. Instead, you see your current step + a *hint* of what's next ("Step 3: Write intro... Next: ???"). This creates a completion drive that pulls you forward without overwhelming you with the full mountain.

**Behavioral principle:** *Open loop psychology* â€” unfinished tasks create cognitive tension (the Zeigarnik effect). By strategically revealing tasks in sequence, Divergify harnesses this tension as motivation fuel.

### F.E.A.R. Framework

**n.** A self-brainwashing system adapted from behavioral influence research: **Focus** (make goals unavoidable via environmental cues), **Emotion** (anchor tasks to feelings/consequences), **Agitation** (create productive urgency), **Repetition** (constant exposure until automatic). F.E.A.R. turns tasks into behavior patterns by hitting your nervous system from multiple angles. Example: Your Anchor Task appears on your lock screen (Focus), you've defined why it matters (Emotion), you see the cost of delay (Agitation), and you get varied reminders every 20 minutes (Repetition).

**Behavioral principle:** *Multi-vector behavior installation* â€” single interventions fail. F.E.A.R. compounds influence by layering environmental, emotional, and repetitive triggers.

### Fractuation

**n.** The art of breaking overwhelming tasks into 2-5 minute micro-steps so tiny your brain can't justify avoiding them. Fractuation eliminates "I don't know where to start" paralysis by defining the absolute smallest next action. Instead of "Write blog post," Fractuation gives you: 1) Open Google Docs, 2) Write one bad sentence, 3) Write two more bad sentences. Each step is so small it feels ridiculous â€” which is exactly why it works.

**Behavioral principle:** *Micro-commitment ladder* â€” large commitments trigger resistance. By reducing the first step to something laughably easy, Fractuation bypasses the brain's "too hard" filter and gets you moving.
